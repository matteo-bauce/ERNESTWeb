About ERNEST

The Project Behind the Game

ERNEST combines science communication, live storytelling, and game-based design to make research feel accessible, social, and memorable for diverse audiences across Europe.

Project overview

Doing science as an adventure.

From concepts to experiences

ERNEST develops scientific accurate escape room scenarios where participants are encouraged to think critically, collaborate, and engage directly with scientific methods and reasoning when facing puzzles and narrative elements grounded in real-world research and contemporary scientific questions. Each activity is designed to make research approachable, hands-on, and memorable, giving audiences a playful way to engage with scientific thinking.

What science

  • Climate-related areas: renewable energy, urban sustainability, atmospheric physics, climate adaptation through environmental risk assessment and Earth observation.

  • Health and cancer research: biomedical physics and health technology innovation.
  • Soil health, food systems and biodiversity.
  • Ocean and water monitoring.
  • Space and astrophysics.
  • Digital transformation: AI, data science and high-performance computing.

Who is it for and where it happens

  • students participating in both escape room sessions and school-based activities.
  • general public and families who want to encounter science in a lively and collaborative format.
Engaging, hands-on science activities are implemented in a wide range of unconventional venues—including classrooms, museums, libraries, historical buildings, public squares, and temporary structures. ERNEST partners bring ERN events directly into the heart of urban life, activating locations that are typically not covered by existing ERN initiatives. The same approach can be applied to researcher-led school activities, bringing the ERNEST atmosphere and objectives to schools and locations that have rarely been engaged before.
