About ERNEST

The Project Behind the Game

ERNEST combines science communication, live storytelling, and game-based design to make research feel accessible, social, and memorable for diverse audiences across Europe.

Project overview

An outreach framework built around curiosity, participation, and collaboration.

Why ERNEST exists

ERNEST was designed to translate complex scientific topics into experiences that feel inviting rather than intimidating. Instead of presenting research only through talks or exhibitions, the project turns learning into a shared mission where players investigate, interpret evidence, solve puzzles, and discuss ideas together.

The escape room format helps create a strong emotional memory: participants are not only told about science, they actively use observation, teamwork, logic, and experimental thinking while moving through a story-driven environment.

Core mission

Connect research communities with teenagers, adults, schools, and the wider public through immersive experiences inspired by physics and astronomy.

What makes it different

ERNEST blends scientific content, scenography, facilitation, and game design into a replicable format that can travel across institutions and public events.

How it works

A project method built on co-design, testing, and public activation.

01

Scientific translation

Research topics are converted into clues, mechanics, and stories without losing rigor.

02

03

Public deployment

Experiences are activated during outreach events, festivals, university programmes, and schools.
